After drifting between different countries and cities for more than thirty years, I finally realized that no place is truly comfortable. The world around us is always changing, and things come and go like fleeting clouds. [The only two constants are change itself and the unpredictability of life.](https://www.scottishpoetrylibrary.org.uk/poem/emfinis-exoptatusem/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}

Decades pass in the blink of an eye. There simply is not enough time to suffer or agonize over external things. Achievements and professional titles are ultimately just a few lines written on paper... Apart from the connections we have with the few people we care about, there is not much else.

I used to think that songs such as [Eric Moo’s “Life Is Like a Dream” (Rénshēng Rú Mèng)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lqnDYKvXQIk){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"} or [Sheena Ringo’s “Life Is Full of Dreams” (Jinsei wa Yume Darake)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GDnLHd2Ei3Y){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"} sounded as though they had been written simply for the sake of writing a song. But as time passes, you gradually begin to understand why artists and poets often compare living to dreaming.

(Text in image:

Sólo venimos a dormir, sólo venimos a soñar:
No es verdad, no es verdad que venimos a vivir en la tierra.

We only came to sleep, We only came to dream:
It is not true, it is not true that we came to live on the earth.

AZTEC POEM)
